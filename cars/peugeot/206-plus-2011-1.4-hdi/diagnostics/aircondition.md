# Aircondition diagnostics

Symptom from owner: A/C becomes cool, but not properly cold compared with another car.

## Important distinction

| System | Danish | English | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| Engine cooling | Kølervæske | Coolant / antifreeze | Cools the engine |
| Aircondition | Aircondition-kølemiddel | A/C refrigerant | Makes cabin air cold |

Changing coolant will not make A/C colder.

## Quick test

1. Put a thermometer in a center air vent.
2. Set A/C on coldest setting.
3. Turn recirculation on.
4. Set blower to medium.
5. Let it run 5 minutes.
6. Record outside temperature and vent temperature.

## Things to check first

- Cabin filter: old/blocked filter reduces airflow.
- Condenser: check front condenser for leaves, dirt, damaged fins.
- Radiator/condenser fan: should normally run when A/C is active.
- Compressor: check if it engages when A/C is turned on.
- Leaks: if refrigerant was added and cooling is weak again, suspect a leak.

## Tools to consider

- Digital thermometer
- UV light if dye is in system
- A/C manifold gauge set
- OBD scanner capable of reading A/C pressure/commands if available

## Do not guess-fill refrigerant

Too little refrigerant cools badly. Too much refrigerant can also cool badly and can damage the system. Use the correct refrigerant type and amount from the label/service data.
